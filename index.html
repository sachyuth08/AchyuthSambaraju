About Me


Hey there! I’m Achyuth Sambaraju—a mechanical engineer turned information systems junkie who’s basically been hoarding skills like they’re clearance rack treasures. Since I was a kid, I’ve had this weird superpower to “see through things”—which, thankfully, landed me in CAD design instead of a sketchy gig as a fortune teller. But I figured out quick that to really stir the pot, I needed to mix in some coding and data wizardry. So, here I am!

I’m slogging through a Master’s in Information Systems at Cal State Long Beach (4.0 GPA, because I’m that guy), where I’ve bamboozled folks into letting me lead teams on wild projects. Picture this: machine learning models sniffing out credit risks during economic trainwrecks (looking at you, COVID), or smart farming apps with IoT—because plants deserve Wi-Fi too, right? I’m the dude who steps up when the chips are down, mostly because I’m a sucker for a challenge (and maybe the occasional pat on the back).

Sports? Oh, I’ve got a highlight reel longer than my skills list—which is saying something. I was the face of my college soccer team—less “Messi,” more “guy who didn’t faceplant the ball”. High jump, gymnastics, swimming, skating—if it involves defying gravity or eating dirt, I’ve done it. Those adventures taught me leadership, teamwork, and how to laugh off a bruised ego, which I now use to survive group projects and life.

Speaking of skills, mine’s a list so long it’s practically a novella—too many to count, and no, I’m not a pro at all of them. But I know enough to bluff my way through anything. Don’t believe me? Check out my #series on Medium, where I spill the beans on life lessons that turned into soft skills I wield daily—like how a botched high jump taught me to bounce back, or how babysitting a team turned me into a people-management fan. Oh, and analytics? I’ve grown to love that number-crunching chaos too.

Here’s the kicker: after all this madness, I’ve realized learning new software or rallying the troops to get stuff done is a breeze for me. And yeah, I used AI to write this—and a bunch of other stuff too. Who doesn’t these days? I say embrace it—use it to stay sharp, present, and irreplaceable. It’s not going anywhere, so start making friends with it. Trust me, they’re pretty cool once you get past the robot vibes.

I’m hooked on companies that mash hardware and software into stuff people adore, and I’m itching to bring my CAD tricks and coding chops to the party. I’m not just hunting for a paycheck—I’m on a quest to build something epic and maybe accidentally save the world (no pressure). I know where I’m headed, and I’m ready to trip, laugh, and hustle my way there.

So, what’s the play? Let’s whip up something extraordinary—or at least have a riot crashing and burning together!
